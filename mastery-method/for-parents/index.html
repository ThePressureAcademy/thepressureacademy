Honest answers

What parents usually want to know

These are the questions parents ask most often — the real ones, not the polished marketing ones.

"I'm not a teacher. Can I really help?"
You don't need to be. The teaching blueprint gives you specific, practical guidance — not lesson plans. You're reinforcing what Kirsty is teaching, not delivering new content. If you can ask a question and listen to the answer, you can do this.
"What if I don't have time?"
Home support is designed to be realistic — think 5-10 minutes, a few times a week. Not another homework session. The clarity framework also improves the quality of time you already spend together, so you don't need more hours — just better ones.
"What if my child pushes back when I try to help?"
That resistance often comes from the child sensing the parent's uncertainty. When you have a clear blueprint, you approach support differently — with calm confidence instead of anxiety. Children respond to that shift. Kirsty will also give you specific strategies for navigating pushback.
"Will this replace what the school is doing?"
No — and it shouldn't. Mastery Method works alongside school, not against it. The clarity framework strengthens the skills that make school work easier. When the school teaches content, your child is better equipped to absorb, process, and apply it.
"How is this more financially sensible than just booking more sessions?"
Because we're building your family's capacity, not your dependence. The teaching blueprint and home support tools mean your child continues progressing between sessions. Over time, your family needs less intensive support — not because we've cut it, but because you're more capable.
"What if my child has a learning difference or diagnosis?"
Mastery Method is designed to work with how your child thinks, not against it. The clarity pillars are adaptable. If your child has a diagnosis, that becomes part of the picture — not something we work around. Mention it on your Clarity Call so we can factor it in from day one.
