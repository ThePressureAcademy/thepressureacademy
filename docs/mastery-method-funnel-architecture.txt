Traditional tutoring re-teaches content. It assumes the
child missed something in class and needs it explained
again. That works when the problem is a content gap.

content. It's clarity.

1. CLARITY OF THINKING
Can they break a question down before they start?

2. CLARITY OF COMMUNICATION

3. CLARITY OF LEARNING
Do they know what success looks like and how to use feedback?

4. CLARITY OF ACTION
Can they start, sustain, and finish work independently?

These four pillars sit beneath every subject. They're the
skills that make Maths, English, Science — everything —
more accessible. And they're what the Clarity Assessment
is designed to identify.

biggest gap. That's not a diagnosis — it's a direction.
It tells us where to look first.

actually use.

STAGE 4 — CLARITY CALL
================================================================

By the time the parent reaches the Clarity Call, they have:
  1. Watched a reel that named their child's pattern
  2. Completed a scorecard that gave them a clarity profile
  3. Read 3 emails explaining the framework and the system
  4. Self-identified their child's weakest pillar

Kirsty's job on the call is NOT to re-explain the system.
It's to:
  - Listen to the parent's specific situation
  - Reference their scorecard results
  - Validate what they're experiencing
  - Recommend the Clarity Assessment as the next step
  - Answer questions about what the Assessment produces

This is a 15-20 minute conversation, not a sales pitch.
The parent is already warm. The scorecard did the selling.
