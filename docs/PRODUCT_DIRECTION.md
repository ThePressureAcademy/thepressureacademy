# Product Direction — The Pressure Academy

**Date:** 19 March 2026
**Status:** Pre-launch — digital product is primary revenue target

## Core Business Truth

The Pressure Academy is a premium, systems-led coaching and wellness business. The first commercial transaction should be the **Pressure Planner** digital product.

## Product: The Pressure Planner

- **What:** A digital planning and performance scoring tool for people managing shift work, training, parenting, and structured routines
- **Who:** Shift workers, grapplers/athletes, parents — anyone managing competing demands on energy and time
- **Status:** ~70% complete (remaining work: recording answers, final editing/packaging)
- **First transaction type:** Digital product purchase or early-access waitlist conversion

## Strategic Priority Order

1. **Launch the digital product** — Pressure Planner is the first revenue event
2. **Capture leads** — email/waitlist for those not ready to buy
3. **Use the ecosystem as authority** — Blueprint, Mastery Method, Pressure Tested support credibility but are not the primary conversion target
4. **Do not over-build platform features** ahead of first revenue

## What the Website Must Do Now

1. Position the Pressure Planner as the headline product above the fold
2. Clearly communicate what the buyer gets
3. Provide a primary CTA (buy / join waitlist / get early access)
4. Use the interactive demo as a sales tool, not just a feature showcase
5. Support the product with ecosystem context (not the reverse)
6. Capture emails with clear intent segmentation

## What the Website Should NOT Do

- Treat the Planner as one of six equal ecosystem nodes
- Lead with ecosystem complexity before showing the product
- Over-invest in admin/platform features before first sale
- Mix in unrelated brands (Pressure Systems, LiftIQ, etc.)
- Fabricate testimonials or legal claims

## Remaining Product Bottlenecks

1. Recording remaining answers (content production, not development)
2. Final editing and packaging
3. Payment integration (Stripe or similar — not yet configured)
4. Delivery mechanism (download link, app access, or gated content)

## Content Hierarchy (Target)

```
1. Hero — Product headline + CTA
2. Product value — What you get, who it's for
3. Interactive demo — Try before you buy
4. Social proof — Real outcomes
5. Ecosystem context — Why TPA is different
6. Pricing / Early access
7. FAQ
8. Email capture / Join
9. Footer
```
