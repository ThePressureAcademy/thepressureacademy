Sample preview for a mid-sized allocation-heavy operation.

This page is built for stage-two LiftIQ conversations where a serious prospect wants to see how decision support could look in a more believable operating environment than a top-level landing page can show.

This is a fictional sample environment built to show how LiftIQ could be used in a mid-sized allocation-heavy operation. It is not a live customer deployment.

Sample environment

What this proof asset is trying to demonstrate.

Dispatch pressure Multiple live jobs, short lead-time changes, and uneven operator availability across one day.
Visible constraints Fatigue, credentials, asset class fit, site access, and timing are shown directly instead of buried in memory.
Recommendation clarity LiftIQ ranks likely options, surfaces hard blocks, and keeps the reasoning visible before a rushed call becomes a scramble.

All people, sites, jobs, and operating details on this page are fictionalised for controlled commercial use. The purpose is to show workflow fit and decision pressure, not to imply a live deployment or customer result.

People pressure

35 personnel, unevenly distributed across shift load.

The sample environment assumes 21 operators, 6 dogmen, 5 riggers, and 3 dispatch or allocation staff across day-start, relief, and shutdown work.

Asset pressure

6 cranes and 3 trucks with class and support dependencies.

The point is not just fleet count. It is matching the right person, the right asset class, and the right site constraints fast enough to trust the call.

Shift pressure

Fatigue and last-minute swaps stay visible.

LiftIQ is strongest when the board still moves but the operator can see why an option is rising, slipping, or blocked before the phone call is made.

Watch list

Pressure signals the allocator should not need to hold in memory.

  • 3 operators above the 44-hour weekly watch threshold.
  • 2 candidates blocked on site-specific access or induction gaps.
  • 1 support truck already committed to a later metro corridor job.
  • 1 crane class conflict if the port lift overruns beyond 08:15.

Why it matters

The value is not a pretty score. It is visible decision support.

  • Ranking is explainable against fit, fatigue, credentials, and timing.
  • Hard blocks surface missing requirements without pretending they are optional.
  • The reasoning stays visible enough for a dispatcher, ops lead, or branch leader to challenge it.
