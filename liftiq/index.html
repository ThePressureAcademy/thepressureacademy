DispatchTalon by Pressure Systems
Founding-partner pilot

Dispatch intelligence for labour and lifting operations.

DispatchTalon helps labour, crane, rigging, plant, transport, and lifting teams structure allocation decisions before dispatch. Import job details, match workers to requirements, review credentials and fatigue, track plant where needed, and keep the audit trail. The dispatcher remains the decision-maker.

DispatchTalon is the new working product name for the dispatch intelligence platform previously developed as LIFTIQ.

Why this matters

Most allocation risk is cumulative.

Rough notes, ticket gaps, fatigue load, double-booked people, plant assumptions, and transport questions each look manageable alone. Together they create scramble unless the workflow is visible before dispatch.

Dispatch blind spot

The board looks stable until one change exposes three assumptions.

DispatchTalon turns crew-fit logic into visible requirements, warnings, rankings, and review notes.

Credential risk

Required tickets get found late.

CredentialGate keeps missing mandatory requirements visible before allocation.

Fatigue visibility

Hours and rest history fragment under pressure.

FatigueGuard surfaces warnings and hard blocks while leaving the decision with the dispatcher.

Who DispatchTalon is for

Two operating modes, one dispatcher-led workflow.

Companies configure DispatchTalon around how they actually allocate work, so labour-only teams do not see plant clutter and plant + labour teams can track real equipment context.

Labour-only mode

For teams allocating people.

Use DispatchTalon for workers, roles, credentials, VOCs, fatigue, availability, schedules, task preferences, SmartRank, audit, metrics, job brief import, and one-off review notes.

Plant + labour mode

For teams allocating people and plant.

Add equipment catalogue, asset register, multiple assets under the same class, plant numbers, crane model, travel state, counterweight gap, transport flags, and road-access review prompts.

Mode-based workflow

Two paths, same review-before-dispatch logic.

1

Labour-only mode

Job brief -> worker requirements -> credentials / VOC -> fatigue and schedule -> SmartRank -> allocation -> audit.

2

Plant + labour mode

Job brief -> equipment / asset requirements -> crane and transport review -> worker requirements -> SmartRank -> allocation -> audit.

Job intake

Rough notes become structured jobs only after dispatcher review.

DispatchTalon can parse pasted text, .txt, and .md briefs into suggested fields, selected catalogue requirements, one-off requirements, schedule/timezone details, and plant or crane review notes where relevant.

Selected requirements Mapped to the company's enabled requirement catalogue.
Suggested requirements Shown for confirmation when the brief mentions items outside setup.
One-off requirements Captured for that job without polluting the company catalogue.
Review flags Visible when crane, counterweight, asset, transport, or access details need human confirmation.
