Current logic path

Current workflow in plain terms.

DispatchTalon turns rough job information into a reviewable allocation path. The dispatcher reviews the data, warnings, ranking, and audit context before confirming the allocation.

1

Set up the company workflow

Choose labour-only or plant + labour mode, then enable the credentials, VOCs, equipment, transport, civil, rail, energy, and asset options the company actually uses.

2

Import workers and job details

Import workers from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data. Create jobs manually or import briefs from pasted text, .txt, and .md.

3

Review requirements before creation

Confirm roles, credentials, VOCs, schedule, timezone, one-off requirements, and plant or crane review notes where relevant.

4

Add plant context where needed

Plant + labour jobs can reference equipment class, plant number, crane model, travel state, counterweight gap, support transport, and road-access review flags.

5

Run SmartRank

SmartRank shows worker fit with CredentialGate, FatigueGuard, schedule, availability, and preference signals.

6

Review warnings and conflicts

Hard blocks, fatigue warnings, schedule conflicts, one-off requirements, and readiness notes stay visible.

7

Confirm or record an override

The dispatcher remains the decision-maker and records an override reason where the choice needs explanation.

8

Review audit trail and metrics

Imports, requirements, asset selections, SmartRank, allocations, overrides, and review events feed the audit trail and pilot metrics.

Current pilot truth

Live pilot console with bounded workflow scope.

  • Labour-only and plant + labour operating modes.
  • Company requirement catalogue, filtered job intake, one-off requirements, and asset register / plant numbers.
  • Worker import, Job Brief Import, timezone-aware scheduling, schedule conflict detection, SmartRank, CredentialGate, FatigueGuard, audit, and metrics.
  • Crane model, travel-state, counterweight gap, support transport, and road-access review flags for plant + labour work.

Boundaries still in place

Dispatcher-led workflow, not autonomous allocation.

  • Dispatcher remains the decision-maker and final confirmer.
  • No payroll, invoicing, quote-builder, maintenance, or Fleet Cost & Care replacement claim.
  • No permit, WHS, legal, compliance, or engineering guarantee claim.
  • No route optimisation, permit automation, external calendar sync, OCR/PDF parsing, or inbox ingestion claim.
Decision boundary: DispatchTalon does not approve permits, engineer lifts, guarantee compliance, or replace dispatcher judgement. It surfaces structured review points before a dispatch decision is confirmed.
Workflow fit

DispatchTalon sits beside dispatch, workforce, and planning workflows.

The pilot workflow connects company setup, worker data, job context, visible checks, allocation confirmation, and audit proof while keeping maturity claims disciplined.

Dispatch Import workers, create jobs, rank worker-fit options, review warnings, and confirm allocations with visible reasoning.
Workforce Credentials, VOCs, fatigue, availability, and preference signals remain explainable.
Audit Allocation-level audit events and pilot metrics sit inside the workflow.
