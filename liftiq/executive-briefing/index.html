What phase-1 should test first: structured intake, recommendation quality, credential confidence, fatigue visibility, schedule conflicts, asset/crane review, audit proof, and dispatcher support.

What the briefing can credibly do

  • Clarify whether DispatchTalon's decision-support model fits your operational reality.
  • Show the current pilot layer and commercial boundaries honestly.
  • Frame what a testing partner, founding partner, or commercial pilot would require in time, access, and internal sponsorship.
  • Surface later-stage architecture questions around external systems without pretending those integrations exist.

What the briefing should not imply

  • No claim that DispatchTalon is ready to replace your current dispatch workflow.
  • No claim that DispatchTalon is integrated into Xero, MYOB, dispatch products, external calendars, GPS / telematics, email inboxes, OCR, or PDF parsing today.
  • No claim that DispatchTalon approves permits, engineers lifts, guarantees compliance, or makes transport legality decisions.
  • No promise of measured ROI before a real pilot exists.
  • No enterprise procurement or security claim beyond the current pilot-stage boundary.
