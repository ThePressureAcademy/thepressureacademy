Request the private briefing if you need a serious view of LiftIQ, not a software pitch.

This briefing is for crane business owners, GMs, and branch leaders who want to understand where LiftIQ fits, what a tightly bounded pilot would require, and where the product should remain disciplined for now.

What the briefing covers

Current product state, module truth, pilot boundaries, integration direction, and the questions that would justify or kill a next step.

Who should request it

Operators with enough dispatch and workforce complexity to care about explainable allocation judgement, not teams looking for a polished enterprise rollout on day one.

Discussion topic

Current product truth

What LiftIQ demonstrates today, what it does not, and how to talk about it without turning a controlled conversation into a trust problem.

Discussion topic

Pilot boundaries

What phase-1 should focus on first: recommendation quality, credential confidence, fatigue visibility, and dispatch support rather than broad automation.

Discussion topic

Phase-2 architecture

Where deeper integration could matter later, including possible operational-system access and client-dependent Xero or MYOB alignment if discovery supports it.

Phase-2 finance direction: LiftIQ is being structured so it can later align with downstream financial and admin systems such as Xero and MYOB, but those pathways are planned only. No live finance-system integration is being claimed on this page.

What the briefing can credibly do

  • Clarify whether LiftIQ's decision-support model fits your operational reality.
  • Show the current demonstration layer and commercial boundaries honestly.
  • Frame what a pilot would require in time, access, and internal sponsorship.
  • Surface later-stage architecture questions around Xero, MYOB, or related systems without pretending those integrations exist.

What the briefing should not imply

  • No claim that LiftIQ is ready to replace your current dispatch workflow.
  • No claim that LiftIQ is integrated into Xero, MYOB, or any dispatch product today.
  • No promise of measured ROI before a real pilot exists.
  • No enterprise procurement or security claim beyond the current concept-demo stage.
