================================================================
WHAT THE CLARITY PATHWAY INCLUDES (MONTHLY)
================================================================

1. UPDATED TEACHING BLUEPRINT
   Format: Written/digital document
   Frequency: Updated monthly (or as milestones are reached)
   Contents:
   - Current focus across the four clarity pillars
   - Where the child is progressing
   - Where attention is still needed
   - Specific home reinforcement guidance for the coming period
   - Curriculum alignment notes (what the school expects, in plain language)
   Framing: "Your family's guide to what's happening, what's next, and how to help."

2. PARENT CHECK-IN CALL (1x per month)
   Format: Live call (video or phone), 15-20 minutes
   Purpose:
   - Review the month's progress across the pillars
   - Answer parent questions
   - Adjust the blueprint based on what's happening at home and school
   - Recalibrate home support activities
   Framing: "A conversation with Kirsty to make sure support stays targeted."
   Note: This is parent-facing, not a teaching session. The child is not required to attend.

3. WEEKLY HOME ACTIVITY GUIDANCE
   Format: Digital (email, PDF, or portal notification)
   Frequency: Weekly
   Contents:
   - One specific focus area for the week (linked to the current clarity pillar priority)
   - One practical activity (5-10 minutes, 2-3x per week)
   - "What to notice" prompt (helps the parent observe progress without testing)
   Framing: "Practical, short activities you can fit into your week — not a second homework load."

4. PARENT RESOURCE LIBRARY / PORTAL ACCESS
   Format: Digital (web-based or simple portal)
   Contents (build over time):
   - Clarity pillar explainers
   - Curriculum visibility guides by year level
   - Home support strategy sheets
   - Conversation prompts for parents
   - Seasonal/term-specific guidance
   Framing: "A growing library of tools and guides, designed for parents — not teachers."
   Note: This starts small and grows. Do not over-promise at launch. Frame as "access to our resource library" without implying hundreds of items on day one.

================================================================
WHAT THE CLARITY PATHWAY DOES NOT INCLUDE
================================================================

- Ongoing 1:1 teaching sessions with the child
  (These are available as a separate premium add-on: "Targeted 1:1 Support")
- Tutoring in specific school subjects
- Homework completion service
- Communication with the child's school on the parent's behalf

================================================================
PREMIUM ADD-ON: TARGETED 1:1 SUPPORT
================================================================

Name: Targeted 1:1 Sessions
Format: Live, teacher-led sessions with the child (via video)
Frequency: Booked as needed, not bundled monthly
Purpose: Direct clarity-building work with the child on specific pillar gaps
Pricing: Per session or in small bundles (e.g., 4-session pack)
Framing: "When your child needs focused, expert-led support on a specific clarity gap."

This is the selective premium layer. It is NOT the default.
The Clarity Pathway is designed to reduce dependence on these sessions over time.

================================================================
OFFER ARCHITECTURE (FULL STACK)
================================================================

STAGE 1 (Launch):
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FREE         Clarity Call (15-20 min, no obligation)     │
│              → Qualifies family, explains the system     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PAID         Clarity Assessment (one-time)               │
│              → Four-pillar diagnostic, detailed report   │
│              → Produces the first Teaching Blueprint      │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ONGOING      Clarity Pathway (monthly subscription)      │
│              → Blueprint updates, check-in call,         │
│                weekly activities, resource library        │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PREMIUM      Targeted 1:1 Sessions (per session/bundle)  │
│              → Selective, strategic, as needed            │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Entry flow:
Clarity Call → Clarity Assessment → Clarity Pathway (+ optional 1:1)

The Clarity Call remains the single front door.
The Assessment is recommended on the call.
The Pathway is introduced after the Assessment as the natural continuation.
1:1 sessions are offered when specific gaps need direct teacher-led intervention.

================================================================
PRICING LOGIC (for Kirsty to decide)
================================================================

Assessment: One-time fee. Should feel substantial enough to signal
serious diagnostic work, but not so high it creates a barrier.
Suggested range: $150-$250 AUD. This produces the first blueprint.

Clarity Pathway: Monthly. Should feel like "expert-guided support
that's more affordable than weekly tutoring." The value comparison
is: 4x weekly tutoring sessions @ $60-80 each = $240-320/month.
Clarity Pathway should sit below that while delivering more continuity.
Suggested range: $97-$149 AUD/month.

Targeted 1:1: Per session. Premium pricing.
Suggested range: $80-$120 AUD per session, or 4-pack at slight discount.

These are frameworks for Kirsty to decide, not locked prices.

BODY (Version 2 — approved for homepage):

Kirsty is a senior Queensland educator with over 15 years of
experience across early childhood and primary education. She
has spent her career learning to read what sits beneath the
surface — the child who knows the answer but can't get it onto
the page, the one who shuts down before they've started, the
one whose confidence disappears after a single correction.
She's also a mother, and she knows what it feels like to want
to help but not have the right map.

She built Mastery Method because she saw the same pattern
repeated across every classroom and every parent conversation:
capable children losing ground — not from a lack of ability,
but from a lack of clarity about what was being asked, where
to begin, and what good was supposed to look like. And she saw
parents on the other side of it, trying everything they could
think of, with no clear way in.

Her approach is calm, specific, and grounded in how children
actually think and develop. She works as closely with parents
as she does with children — translating what the school expects
into support that's understandable, practical, and manageable
at home. Not more noise. A clearer signal.
